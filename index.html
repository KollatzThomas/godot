Jewish Calendar

  • lunisolar calender
  • cycle of 19 years used to bring lunar and solar year in common (metonic cycle)
    • a month begins with the new moon
    • festivals are related to seasons: Pessach – the festival to remember the Exodus from Egypt – took place in Spring

years, months, days

leap years seven leap years in the years 3, 6, 8, 11, 14, 17, and 19
In a leap year a second month of Adar is inserted
days/month a month has 29 or 30 days
days/year a normal year has 353, 354 or 355 days a leap year has 383, 384 or 385 days

Liturgical Calendar

The Hebrew Calendar is a liturgical Calendar.
The following elements of Jewish religious life are calendar-based:

  • the reading of the weekly Tora portions (פרשת השבואע). Lectio continua during the year. From time to time weeks are addressed by the title of the weekly portion: הלך לישיבה של מעלה ליל ש״ק פרשת שקלים ך״ז אדר ראשון שנת תמ״ח לפ״ק (bns-3024)
  • the Jewish holidays.
  • Yahrzeit, the day of death is commemoratated according to the Hebrew calendar.
